Liquid Penetrant Level 1 & 2

Our Liquid Penetrant courses meet the 4 hour requirement for Level 1 and the additional 8 hour requirement (12 total) for Level 2 in accordance with ASNT SNT-TC-1A. We can adjust the class material to meet the requirements of MIL STD 410 and NAS 410/EN4179 which requires 16 hours for Level 1 and 16 hours for level 2. This course does not fulfill all requirements for certification. An experience requirement under the guidance of a Level 2 or 3 is required to fulfill all requirements in order to be eligible for certification. This course does offer some hands on instruction. Level 1 and 2 course maybe combined into a single 12 or 32 hour class. The Level 1 class (4 or 16 Hours) covers the history, purpose and basic principles of liquid penetrant testing. It also covers the processing of the different types of penetrant and the methods for qualification of personnel. The Level 2 Class (8 or 16 hours) covers reviews the information for the Level I course and discusses the advantages and disadvantages of the different types of penetrant. A major aspect of this course is the inspection and evaluation of indications..

Magnetic Particle Level 1 & 2

Our Magnetic Particle courses meet the 12 hour requirement for Level 1 and the additional 8 hour requirement (20 total) for Level 2 in accordance with ASNT SNT-TC-1A. We can adjust the class material to meet the requirements of MIL STD 410 and NAS 410 / EN4179 which requires 16 hours for Level 1 and 16 hours for level 2. Some math skills are required. This course does not fulfill all requirements for certification. An experience requirement under the guidance of a Level 2 or 3 is required to fulfill all requirements in order to be eligible for certification. This course does offer some hands on instruction. Level 1 and 2 course maybe combined into a single 20 or 32 hour class. The Level 1 class (12 or 16 Hours) covers the basic principles and characteristics of magnets and magnetic fields. It also covers how to select the proper method of magnetization, the basic equipment used and different types of discontinuities that can be detected through magnetic particle inspection. The Level 2 class (8 or 16 Hours) begins with a review of the information from the Level I course. It then covers the basic principles and theories of magnetic particle inspection and an in depth look at the equipment used in various methods of testing. It also includes types of discontinuities, evaluation techniques as well as evaluation and interpretation of defects

Radiation Safety

Our Radiation Safety Inspection courses meet the 40 hour requirement for radiographers to work safely with radio Isotopes. Math skills are required. This course does not fulfill all requirements for certification. An experience requirement under the guidance of a Radiographer and or Radiation Safety Office is required to fulfill all requirements in order to be eligible for certification.
